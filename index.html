Line 9

Line 9 opened in 1922 initially running between Trocadero and Exelmans before extending to Chausee d'Antin in the east and Porte de Saint-Cloud in the southwest. It was then merged with Line 8 in 1928 at Richelieu - Drouot, and vastly expands to Porte de Montreuil in 1933. It became the first line to extend outside the city, specifically Boulogne-Billancourt in 1934 to Pont de Sevres, and then Porte de Montreuil in 1937. A proposed extension to Montreuil Hopital is yet to be approved, and shall be completed by 2030 if so.

There is an abandoned station on the line that shares with Line 8, Saint-Martin, closed during WW2 in 1939, and never reopened because it was too close to Strasbourg - Saint-Denis, which is only 100 meters west.

Currently there's 37 stops on the line across 19.6km (12.2mi).

Line 10

Line 10 is the least used line in the whole metro apart from Lines 3bis and 7bis, just 40 million people use it every year. It opened in 1923, initially between Invalides and Croix-Rouge, before extending to Mabillon in 1924, and Odeon in 1925. It extended vastly to Porte de Choisy in 1930, until the next year, in 1931, when Line 7 extends from Sully-Morland to take over the segment from Place-Monge to Porte de Choisy, and was amended to Jussieu instead. In 1937, the segment from Invalides to Duroc was taking over by the old line 14, and it was amended to La Motte - Picquet Grenelle where it will take over the old Line 8 to Auteuil. It would then get extended for 1.3km to meet Gare d'Austerlitz in 1938, before WW2. The segment beyond Javel - Andre Citroen would operate as an anti-clockwise loop, until 1981, when the line was extended to Boulogne, making westbound trains head north via Porte d'Auteuil and eastbound trains head south via Mirabeau.

An abandoned station, Croix-Rouge, one of the first termini of the line, closed in 1939 during WW2 and never reopened because it was too close to Sevres-Babylone. Another station that didn't reopen right after the war was Cluny - La Sorbonne, but it reopened in 1988 as its new name to connect with the RER B.

An extension to Ivry-Gambetta running parallel to the RER C is also being proposed, target for completion by 2028.

Line 11

Line 11 is currently the shortest line in the Paris Metro apart from Lines 3bis and 7bis, at just 6.3km (3.9mi). It also has the least number of stations apart from Lines 3bis and 7bis, tied with Line 14 at just 13 stations. It is the 2nd least used line in the network behind Line 10, at just 47 million passengers a year. It opened in 1935 to replace the Belleville funicular and extend to Chatelet and Porte des Lilas, making a configuration of 12 stops. A one-stop extension to Mairie des Lilas opened later in 1937. Currently, a 6-stop extension to Rosny is being built and another extension as part of the Grand Paris Express along with the newer MP14 rolling stock is to be opened towards Noisy-Champs in 2030 where it will link Lines 15 and 16.

Line 12

Line 12 first opened in 1910 as Line A of the now-defunct Nord-Sud Company, between Notre-Dame-de-Lorette and Porte de Versailles. The Nord-Sud Company was a competing railway company to the CMP between the 1910s and 1930s. They envisioned to build more direct railways than what CMP is doing by using the greathead method to bury into the earth to dig deep underground tunnels without disturbing the surface. However, the ground conditions of Paris made building a tunnel impossible to withstand cost effectiveness, so they amended the method to be like the CMP. The company was going to build Line A, now Line 12, initially between Notre-Dame-de-Lorette and Porte de Versailles; Line B, now part of Line 13, initially between Saint-Lazare and the gates in Clichy and Saint-Ouen in two branches, both completed in 1910 and 1911, respectively. The company was going to build a Line C too between Montparnasse and Porte de Vanves, but it was absorbed by the CMP in 1930 due to unsustainable cost situations, making Line A become Line 12 as it is today. Until its defuncting in 1930, Line A was extended to Jules Joffrin in 1912 and Porte de la Chapelle in 1916. After becoming Line 12, it extended into the southern suburbs in 1934 to Mairie d'Issy. The next extension to Line 12 would not happen until 78 years later, in 2012, when it extended to Front Populaire. It was extended once more recently on 31 May 2022, when it extended for two more stops to Mairie d'Aubervilliers.

Two extensions are proposed for Line 12 for now, a northern extension to La Courneuve RER B, and a southern one to Issy RER station.

Line 13

Line 13 opened in 1911 as Line B of the Nord-Sud Company, linking Saint-Lazare with the gates at Saint-Ouen and Clichy. Another segment amalgamated into today's Line 13 is the old Line 14, running between Bienvenue and Porte de Vanves, opened in 1937 as supposed to be Line C of the Nord-Sud Company. Line 13 then extends north to Carrefour Pleyel in 1952, and reaches Miromesnil in 1973. In 1976, it reaches Basilique Saint-Denis and Champ-Elysees-Clemenceau, before reaching Invalides to merge with the old Line 14 and extend to Chatillon Montrouge. In 1980, the northwestern branch was extended to Gabriel Peri, and the northeastern branch to Universite de Saint-Denis in 1998, and finally the northwestern branch gets extended to Les Courtilles in 2008.

A northwest extension to Porte de Gennevilliers is being considered as well as a northeastern extension beyond Saint-Denis Universite
